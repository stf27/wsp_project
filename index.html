Target Audience

The target audience for Fhoke is any business (most likely small) looking to create their website. I don't believe there is any preference towards a gender for their target audience. There seems to be a target age for their audience however. The design tells me that they are a young modern design firm looking to help business owners from their 20s up to middle aged people. In other words, they are not targeting people too young to work or people that have retired. They are looking for clients that are business owners.

Client and User Needs

The focus of this company is to design a website for their client. The client goals/needs of the project would be three things; to earn revenue, to create positive customer service (make the client happy), and to increase their customer base/become more well known. The user goal would be to come out with a great looking product that conveys the style and appeal their target audience is looking for.

Critique

The high contrast between bright and dark colors for this component create a modern in your face look and feel. The red background grabs the user's attention while the black type makes it easily read. They component employs the use of a sans-serif font feeding more into that modern design that they are going for. On the left is the company name designed with custom typography successfully creating an image/branding style. On the right are the main navigational points of the page. They are layed out in a line to create a streamlined and efficient user experience.

Call to Action

Target Audience

BIXBI is a company that makes dog treats. Their target audience is dog owners. I don't believe that they are targeting a particular gender. They are going for the modern hip look while creating healthy snacks for your dog. Because of the style and design of their treats, I believe the target age for their audience is young adults (people in their 20s and 30s). However, because of their claim to be natural and healthy, their audience can really be any age.

Client and User Needs

The client needs for this product are to increase revenue, build quality customer service/feedback, and reach out to a larger audience. They hope to create a health product that pets and their owners will love. The user needs are much more basic. They need a trustworthy product that they know is healthy and their dog will enjoy.

Critique

The bright colors of this CTA immediately draw attention to itself. The background is yellow which creates a sense of energy and an overall just do it kind of feel (perfect for a CTA). The type is large and bold which helps to draw attention to itself as well. The words are separated by dashed lines creating a sense of hierarchy. The size and layout of the type also adds to this hierarchy. The component also employs a picture of a dog (who appears to be pointing) to draw attention to the information the CTA is presenting. They include a picture of their product as well. It is the same color as the background of the CTA which creates a sense of branding and continuity. At the bottom of the component is a bright orange purchase now button. It is placed after the useful information the CTA presents which creates the idea of, "Hey, we have an awesome product. We know you are sold on it. Here is how you can buy it right now."

Contact

Target Audience

Creative Department is another design agency. Their target audience is small businesses looking to create friendly approachable branding. They do not have a target gender for their work. The target age for their audience is people old enough to work at/own a business.

Client and User Needs

The client needs for Creative Department are the same as most design agencies. Blog

Target Audience

POSHCSS is a web blog targeting web designers. They post stories from many different sources with information on web design. There is no target gender for POSHCSS. The title of their site, "For the modern day web designer," leads me to believe that the target age of their audience is younger web designers. But based off of the information that they post, their audience could be any web designer, young or old.

Client and User Needs

The central client need for POSHCSS is community outreach. They are a website dedicated to spreading information about web design. Their main focus is creating quality content and sharing it with as many people as possible. The user needs information and the latest news on web design. They are looking for quality content with useful information.

Critique

The purpose of this blog is simplicity and ease of use. It accomplishes just that. The colors are very simple; a white background with black text. The links are blue to let the user know it can be clicked (adding to the ease of use). There is a very nice hierarchy set up as well. The user is presented with a big bold title for the website's name and a smaller type describing what the website is for. There are two black lines below that to create a separation from the title and the content of the site. The type is then broken up into different sizes to distinguish between the title of the piece and the content itself.

News Feed

Target Audience

Google+'s news feed is designed to deliver the latest news from your friends and companies that you follow. Their target audience is everyone, people of any age and companies of any kind. The purpose of a social media site is to reach as many people as you can no matter race, gender, or age.

Client and User Needs

The client needs for Google+ are creating positive customer service but more importantly, creating a larger community. They want as many people and businesses to use their service as possible. All of this translates to the idea of giving the user the news they want and doing so as simply and as quickly as possible.

Footer

Target Audience

This is a personal website for a designer by the name Jarad Johnson. It leads you to two companies that he co-founded. One is a design firm and the other a web hosting service. The target audience is anyone looking for those services. There is not target gender. The target age of their audience is generally going to be younger people since the design is built more around the modern look. But really people of any age can use these services.

Client and User Needs

The client needs for this website is to create a larger community outreach for the two companies that he co-founded. The site is there just to create more awareness. The user needs are also simple. They are visiting the site for information. Information on the person or information the his companies.

Critique

This components is designed for simplicity. It has a white background with grey images linking to this person's social media pages. The images are lined up in a single row, adding to the simplicity, and creating an easy to read quick to figure out footer. Below the images is a line created to separate (hierarchy) the social media links and the text below it. The text is the same light grey color as that social media images (creating continuity). The font is a serif however. This goes against the idea of simplicity and ease of use (since serif fonts are generally a bit harder to read), but I feel is appropriate since this is a personal website and not a company website. Generally companies like to use sans-serif fonts because they are clean and create a sense of reliability and honesty. While personal websites like to use serif fonts because it makes them more "human" and more personal.
